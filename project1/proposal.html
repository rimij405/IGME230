Olympia 2608!

A 2D hack n' slash, with a neon, futuristic look, inspired by the cybernetic Olympic games, in the year 2608.

Game Treatment

In order to showcase the power of PIXI.js, project 3 has evolved from a simple typewriting game (a la Mavis Beacon), now will challenge the developer to create a more visually satisfying interactive experience.

High Concept

History repeats itself; like the Roman collesiums of old, cybernetic gladiators will duke it out in the post-postmodern neon world games series: Olympia Rising, in the year of 2608.

Genre

A 2D hack n' slash with an emphasis on speed and accuracy.

Platform

Browser only.

Story

In the year 2608, the competitive Olympia Rising games take place, pitting cyborg against cyborg. Players will take the role of their own cyborg, who is tasked with surviving in a free for all arena against other players, in order to take back to their hover-home the synthetic gold medal.

Aesthetic

Drawing heavily on the branding from past Summer Olympic Games, the game parodies the feel of the real games, except, only if they had taken place in a neon-filled, cybernetic future. (Think a more hopeful, less sepia-tone filled Deus Ex Machina). Since the game is 2D, using a cartoonish artstyle can highlight the lighthearted and fun nature of the game. While the player is competitive, it will communicate to them that nothing is at stake - there is no risk.

The soundscape can be compared to Final Fantasy X's score during the segment of the game in which the characters play the exciting and highly watched blitzball. In other words, a thrilling musical score, with fanfare and trumpets, to make the player feel like they are at a prestigious athletic event.

Gameplay

The following section covers the gameplay portion of the document.

Mechanics

Combat is the core of the game, and, it's quite simple. Cyborgs must angle themselves in the direction they want to go, and 'dash n' slash' in order to cause damage to their enemies. If two cyborgs dash at each other at the same time, damage to both is havled, and the cyborgs will bounce back. Below are some possible game modes.

Last Stand

Players will compete against other cyborgs with their hack n' slash techniques, to be the last cyborg alive in the arena.

Hold 'Em

Players must collect several items that are scattered around the arena, in order to create a torch. Cyborgs can steal pieces from other cyborgs. Once all the pieces are owned, the cyborg must race to the finish line to light the torch.

Capture the Flag

Players will fight to capture a flag from the center of the field and return it to their starting location. The first cyborg to 3 points will win.

Controls

Onboarding

Players will be able to find the instructions in an interactive tutorial section, clearly marked from the main menu, in the game.

Player Learning

Players will need to learn how to keep track of elements on the screen, avoid enemies, and strategize plans.

Players who are unable to keep track of what's on the screen will be unable to win.
